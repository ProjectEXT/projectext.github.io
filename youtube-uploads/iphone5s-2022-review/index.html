Buying an iPhone 5S in 2022: Is It Worth It?

Jan 4, 2022

Introduction

Another day, another iphone from eBay. This time though, this device was bought from a trusted buyer that I’ve good experience with in the past.

only costing me roughly $20 free shipping. If the item was considered to be in good condition, they’d normally sell for $60 (That’s a big price difference). This listing though, stated there were issues with the LCD (the screen). Looking at all of the photos, there aren’t any signs that I can see that would concern me about issues with the LCD screen & figured maybe they’re just being picky with their item conditions & wanting to be more safe than sorry when selling.

Unboxing

It came in a plain-white box with little to no packaging to protect the device inside, but it didn’t seem to get damaged so that’s good. Immediately, I scrubbed this thing down with some lysol wipes, this device looked like it was ran over by a country-side farmer or something. I’d love to know the story behind this device. Most of the icky goo stuff didn’t come off on the first scrub, but I got the rest off after recording.

Trying to turn on the device after plugging it into my powerbank, it wouldn’t turn on whatsoever. This is a well-known issue with apple devices that have poor battery life & require the wall-power’s UHMPF to get it to spark to life.

Initial Boot

When the device decided to show it cared about me, first thing I noticed was it’s already been through the setup process. Checking to make sure nobody’s account was still logged in, the screen looks like a vintage TV - if any of you remember what those look like, they probably looked better than this in white light. It’s not as noticeable when not using white-colored applications, but there’s definitely some burn-in. Comparing it to the iPhone SE from my last video, the iPhone 5s’s age is starting to show.

Capacity is 16gb which matches the ebay listing so that’s good, and running ios 12.1.3 which isn’t the latest so thats kind of cool. For testing out the speakers, we gave our friendly internet-neighbor Rick a good visit, sounding fresher than ever. The headphone jack even works this time!

The Test of Time

I wanted to see if the device would die or become worse overtime, so I decided to use it as a mediaplayer, spotify listener for a couple weeks. I had a little scare the first week…. Turns out it was my tablet, guess it’s good I don’t use it often. Overall though, it works amazingly for what it is, a 7 year old phone at this point. Don’t expect it to outmatch, or even compare to any newer device in 2021 - it shouldn’t be used for anything more than basic tasks like phone calls & listening to music etc. With that said though, it’s still a beefy phone especially for it’s size & age.

Besides the slightly burned-in screen, I can’t see any reason to mark this item listing as “for parts or not working”. The seller could be making lots more profit from their listings, but maybe they know that & want to give these phones to good homes knowing anybody buying parts are probably worth selling them to. I might add the ebay seller in the description if you’re looking to pickup yourself any classic phones or devices & want to find one at a great price in pretty good condition.
