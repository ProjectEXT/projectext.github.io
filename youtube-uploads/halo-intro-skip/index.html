How to Change Halo Infinite’s Long Loading Screen

Jan 5, 2022

So awhile back, a couple months ago by the time I got around to writing this script, I posted a random video about Halo Infinite’s annoyingly long intro on my second channel. I didn’t put lots of effort into the video, even though I knew it would be gain some traction because at the time I was pretty sick and couldn’t care. My only real regret is not putting it on the main channel.

Well, one of the comments asked, how do I do this myself? They actually just said how, but that doesn’t make sense out of context. It’s really easy, so I thought I’d make a tutorial for those still dealing with the obnoxiously long unstoppable cut scene.

First thing you’re going to want to do is find a video you want to replace it with. Easiest way is just heading to youtube, copying the video link & pasting it into a youtube-to-mp4 website. I’ll paste the website I used in the video description, but it’s really trial and error with them, sometimes one website works wonderful, other times you can’t find one that will work at all.

After that, you’re going to want to rename the video file to “intro” all lowercase. This is how Halo Infinite recognizes and loads in the original mp4 file, glad to see there’s tamper protection from users like us changing it.

Next is to navigate to your Halo infinite game directory, by now most of you should know how to do this. If not, the most common location is:

C:\Program Files (x86)\Steam\steamapps/Halo Inifnite

Inside, open the videos folder which contains most UI cut scenes.

From there, it’s just drag and drop. Replace the old file with the new, and bam your game is now modified and you’re going to go to jail. Thats it, simple and easily customizable. It’s fun to see what people can come up with, so please let me know what you come up with in the comments, I could use a good laugh.

Now that I think about it, I should’ve really used a rick roll for this video tutorial.
