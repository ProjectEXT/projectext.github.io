Sonic Adventures 2 Battle on PC - Is it BAD?

Oct 11, 2021

Introduction

Sonic adventure 2 was born & released on June 19, 2001 in NA, making this game almost old enough to drink. Quick disclaimer, this video isn’t anything like a review, I won’t be going in depth about its gameplay or anything like that. With that out of the way, lets talk about what this game holds & how it holds up in 2021.

Sonic Adventure 2 is kind of known to be one of the best 3D sonic games, which doesn’t say much for the rest of the series. I first played this game when I was like 4, and it was the GameCube version. I LOVED this game. I didn’t know what the hell I was doing, but I knew there were these eggs that if I YEETED across the map, a weird looking baby thing would pop out.

Years later, I’m bored scrolling through steam & see both the game & its DLC which we’ll talk more about later in the video, were on sale for around $3.50 all-together. Knowing me, I wanted to relive my childhood. Now that I’m not a cute lil baby anymore & I’m an ugly dumbass that acts like an aging nanny from the nursing home, the “polish”, if you can call it that, has rubbed off just a tad.

Performance Issues & Bugs Galore

Just trying to open the game with default settings, the game will crash. Every time. Yeah, turns out you can’t open the game with fullscreen enabled. When getting into the game, something seemed wrong with the first cutscene, but I didn’t notice what it was until I got into the gameplay itself.

Let me explain. Even though the settings claim to be 2560x1440 @ 60fps, its actually running at native 144hz. This game was built around the time in-game sequences & events were based around the amount of frames the game will be played at. If you don’t understand what I mean, I will leave a video in the description better explaining. This explains why cutscenes were COMPLETELY Broken at times. Because of this game mechanic, everything I do in the game will be sped up way more than the game is meant to be played.

The solution to fixing this problem isn’t difficult, just annoying. You have to limit the frame counter to 60fps through Nvidia’s control panel, adding the game as a program & tinkering with its settings in there. Don’t ask me what you’d have to do if you didn’t have a nvidia card, god speed. Changing this setting, the game ran fine afterword’s.

Finishing up playing for the night, I tried playing the game the next morning and noticed my controller was no longer working. It’s always fun sitting around, reading through a dozen articles on reddit trying to find a solution, just to see that the game launcher decided to switch my default controller input to keyboard. Thanks.

DLC

I’m not going to talk about the story or characters, as I’m not a sonic fanatic & haven’t completed it fully just yet. I do plan to though, so if you want to see a review of such please lemme know in the comments below. I WILL however, talk about how the only DLC for this game is literally to allow you to have your “chaos” fight eachother to the death… or at least to the end. It’s Karate fights that can level up your “creatures” & show off what you’ve turned them into. According to steam, it adds six new characters, and 21 additional 2 player maps but nobody cares, we’re here for the CPS Violations!

Conclusion

Having widescreen is nice, and playing at 60fps over 30fps is the dream, I think leaving this game in the past & with lil baby me would’ve been the better way to go. The game’s littered with classic bugs here and there, but that would be apart of the story & i’m not reviewing that in this video. Anyways, (outro). Apparently my Sister watches my videos now…. this one will be hard to explain. PEACE!
